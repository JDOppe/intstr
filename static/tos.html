Integer Stealer Privacy Policy and Terms of Use

Last Updated: April 19th, 2024.

Section 1: Definitions

In this privacy policy, "We" refers to "Integer Stealer". "Personally-identifiable information" (PII) refers to information that can be used to identify a person such as IP addresses. "Official Integer Stealer deployments" refer to the Integer Stealer application running on the official server. "Integer Stealer deployment operator" refers to the person(s) running the Integer Stealer application. "ToS" refers to Terms of Service.

Please redirect any general inquiries to our github page. Redirect any questions regarding unofficial Integer Stealer deployments to its operator.

Section 2: Summary

  • By using Integer Stealer, you agree you understand and acknowledge this privacy policy and its clauses. This page has been created to help you understand what types of data we collect, how we use and keep your data safe, etc. If you do not agree with this privacy policy and terms of service, you must cease use of our services.
  • Integer Stealer will not be held liable in any situation that may occur as a result of using or hosting the Integer Stealer application on ANY Integer Stealer deployment.
  • The safest way to use Integer Stealer is by using its official deployments or by hosting Integer Stealer on your own machine >.

Section 2.1: Summary - Offical Integer Stealer Deployment

Official Integer Stealer deployments are run with privacy in mind. We do temporarily collect and retain some PII such as IP addresses used to access the official Integer Stealer deployment, but this is not intentional.

Section 2.2: Summary - Unofficial Integer Stealer Deployments

  • Integer Stealer developers are commited to ensuring Integer Stealer remains as a secure application. However, we cannot guarantee the same level of security or privacy of unofficial Integer Stealer deployments.
  • The type of information collected on community deployments is completely dependent on who and where the deployment is being hosted.
  • Generally, we recommend Integer Stealer deployment operators to collect as minimal information as possible.
  • We cannot control unofficial Integer Stealer deployments.
  • We cannot verify the authenticity of the code in unofficial Integer Stealer deployments. This is an extremely rare situation, but we will not be held responsible if your data such as an account becomes compromised as a result of malicious code in an unofficial Integer Stealer deployment.

Section 5: The information we collect

Section 5.1: The official Integer Stealer deployment

  • Information that may be unintentionally collected as part of using Integer Stealer's official deployment may include basic information such as your IP address, web traffic when using Integer Stealer, etc. This information is temporary and is kept until the server is rebooted.
  • We do not purposefully collect anyone's information. We try to limit the data collected by the official Integer Stealer deployment.
  • We do not, will not, and will never knowingly collect the information of minors under the age of 13 (or the minimum age required by laws in your country).

Section 5.2: Unofficial Integer Stealer Deployments

  • We can't give a definitive answer on what information is collected when you use a unofficial Integer Stealer deployment, only the operator of the Integer Stealer deployment you're using can.
  • We urge operators to collect and store as minimal information as possible, but we can't control what they do.

Section 6: Control

  • We do not have the power to control what unofficial Integer Stealer deployment operators do with their deployment.
  • We protect information stored on the official Integer Stealer deployment by limiting access. The only person that has access to the server of which the official Integer Stealer deployment runs on is the server host and the owner of Integer Stealer, xbubbo.
  • We cannot check every deployment for malicious code. If any data such as your account gets compromised after you access it on a unofficial Integer Stealer deployment, we will not be held liable.

Section 7: How we use basic information on the official Integer Stealer deployment that may be collected accidentally

  • Your IP address is used to allow you to use the Integer Stealer service. This is how it works for every website you visit, not just Interstellar. The difference is whether or not this data is intentionally collected and stored.
  • Info about your web traffic while using Integer Stealer is needed to allow you to access the service you wish to access.
  • Integer Stealer will NEVER sell your info to third-parties, other than to some ad companies. We do this so we can make enough money to pay for our server.
  • The official Integer Stealer server operator may respond to legal requests and assist law enforcement officials in legal investigations if ever needed. We can't guarantee we'll always have information to hand out as the information that may be collected is automatically destroyed on reboot, but if we do still have information relevent to the investigation, we may provide law enforcement officials with it. Please redirect all requests regarding data with an unofficial Integer Stealer deployment to its operator, as we don't have control over unofficial deployments and couldn't comply with requests even if we wanted to.
